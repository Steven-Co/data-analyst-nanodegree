Ford GoBike Usage Habits By Gender and Age¶

Investigation Overview¶

For this investigation, I examined Ford GoBike data to see how members of different genders and age groups are represented and how they use the service. This exploration covered number of rides per age and gender, age makeup by gender, ride times, distance, and ride duration.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset contained information pertaining to around 1,750,000 rides during 2018. The information covered areas including rider gender, age, time of ride, ride duration, starting and ending location, and other details including bike id, type of user, station name, and more. 111,594 results were removed due to missiong or presumably inaccurate information.

Distribution by Gender¶

The gender breakdown of rides by gender leans heavily towards males with over 1.2 million rides. Rides by females account for around 400,000 rides while those identifying as other number around 27,000.

Distribution by Birth Year¶

Ages in the GoBike data begin steadly rising for birth years from around 1960 to 1980 where we see a steep increase peaking around the year 1989. These numbers begin to trail off for birth years in the 90's until it stops at what is presumably the latest year, the year 2000, a user is allowed to signup due to age restrictions. The data takes on a unimodal shape.

Birth Year Distriubtion by Gender¶

Birth year distribution by gender shows that the age distribution among these groups is relatively similar with the highest concentration taking place in the 80's. Females tend to have slightly younger riders than the other two groups (mostly between 1980 and 1991). Males have the largest interquartile range which extending from the late 70's to the early 90's. The other group ages appear to fit somewhere the concentrations of male and female ages.

Average Ride Time by Gender¶

Plotting the average duration of ride times by gender reveals that females a likely to have longer rides than males or members within the other group. While males make up and overwhelming number of rides that take place in the GoBike service, their average ride duration (around 733 seconds) is considerably less that of females (roughly 901 seconds). As with the age distribution, users within the other category fit in-between these groups with an average ride time of around 867 seconds.

Ride Volume Per Day for Each Gender¶

By observing the ride volume per day across all genders, we can infer that Monday - Friday is the most popular time for making use of the GoBike service. These figures reach their highest points towards the middle of the week and drop shaprly during the weekend. This may be due to the GoBike service being used during work commutes, but there is not enough information in the data fully support this claim.

Ride Distance Between Start an End Stations By Gender and Age¶

In plotting ride distance in miles between starting and ending bike stations, we can observe that higher distance concentractions (marked by darker coloration) occur at or below 3 miles. Additionally, the most of these trips appear to be made by younger riders with birth years in the 80s and onward. Males tend to have the most broad range of distances followed by women and other. Additionally the age range of male riders appears to extend more evenly (without breaks and further back) than the other two gender groups.
